## Category Analysis

### Media apps (The Berrics, Thrasher, Braille)

Content-first platforms built around professional or semi-professional skate media. They produce and distribute video content but offer no competitive gameplay, no user-vs-user interaction, and no structured progression system. Users are viewers, not players. There is no game loop that drives daily engagement or retention beyond content consumption.

### Discovery apps (Shred Spots, Skatepark Project)

Spot-finding tools that help skaters locate parks and street spots. Useful utilities, but they have no social layer, no competition mechanics, and no reason to return daily once you've found the spots near you. They solve a one-time lookup problem, not an engagement loop.

### Social platforms (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube)

Massive audiences and strong skate communities, but zero structured competition. Skaters post clips for likes and comments — there's no way to challenge someone, no judging system, no win/loss record, and no reputation that compounds over time. These platforms own the audience but not the competitive identity.

### SkateHubba

The only entry combining **competition** (async S.K.A.T.E. battles with video proof), **discovery** (spot map with geo-verified check-ins), and **identity** (skate graph capturing tricks, outcomes, reputation, and crews). This combination creates a daily engagement loop — Watch, Battle, Check-in, Share, Repeat — that no single-category app can replicate.

---

## Indirect Competitors

- **Tony Hawk's Pro Skater / Skate (EA):** Entertainment gaming, not real-world skating. Different audience, different value prop. SkateHubba is about real tricks by real skaters.
- **Sports competition apps (e.g., PlayVS, Sleeper):** Similar async competition model applied to other sports/esports. Validates the category but none target skateboarding's unique culture and mechanics.
- **Strava / fitness apps:** Demonstrate that activity tracking + social competition drives strong retention in physical sports. SkateHubba applies this model to skateboarding with video proof instead of GPS data.

---

## Defensibility

The **skate graph** is the moat. Every battle, every judgement, every check-in, every crew affiliation adds data that makes the platform more valuable:

- **Clip graph:** Tricks linked to spots, skaters, and outcomes
- **Reputation graph:** Win/loss records, judging accuracy, trust scores
- **Spot graph:** Check-in frequency, trick density, local scenes
- **Crew graph:** Team affiliations, crew rivalries, regional identity

Each layer compounds. A new entrant would need to rebuild not just the product but the entire history of battles, judgements, and reputation — the same cold-start problem that protects every successful social network.
